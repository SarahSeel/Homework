History of Unicorns

    April 9th is marked as National Unicorn Day!
    Unicorns were described in the 1st century CE as: "Head of a stag, feet of an elephant, tail of a boar, and the rest of the body like a horse. It has a single black horn projecting for the middle of its forehead."
    "Writings in the 6th century CE, unicorns are notorious to avoid capture. The power of its horn gives an advantage to escape unharmed."

Unicorns in Medieval Time

  • Over time the Christian church symbolized unicorns as a symbol of purity and grace.
  • A Carthaginian author (Tertullian) symbolized the unicorn as a symbol for Christ as it's horn represented the cross. As it's represents "glory, power, and salvation".
  • In the Middle Ages, tapestries called "The Unicorn Tapestries" pictured different representations of unicorns and their abilities. Examples include that Unicorns could only be surrounded by virgins since they were pure creatures. They could defend itself from harms way with it's horn. It's horn also had curing properties from poison.
  • European family crests often would choose a unicorn to represent their house due to their "ability to cure poison and danger".
